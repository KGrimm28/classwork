LeBron James

LeBron James is an American basketball superstar in the National Basketball Association (NBA). He was drafted out of St. Vincent-St. Mary High School in 2003 to the Cleveland Cavaliers. He left Cleveland in 2010 to go to the Miami Heat. He won two consecutive championships in Miami in 2012-2013. He returned to Cleveland in 2014 and won Cleveland their first championship in 2016 ending a title drought.

Early Life

On December 30, 1984, LeBron Raymone James was born to Gloria Marie James, who was only 16-years-old, in Akron, Ohio. James was introduced to basketball when he was nine years old. His mother wanted him to live in a family environment, so he lived with Frank Walker (a youth football coach).

James began playing for the Amateur Athletic Union (AAU) for the Northeast Ohio Shooting Stars. He and three others on the team were known as the "Fab Four."

James later attended St. Vincent-St. Mary High School. He averaged 21 points and 6 rebounds as a freshmen. As a sophomore, he averaged 25.2 points and 7.2 rebounds. James was so good that St. Vincent-St. Mary played at University of Akron's Rhodes Arena for alumni, fans, and college and NBA scouts to watch him play. James was named Ohio Mr. Basketball. James was also featured on Sports Illustrated covers. James wanted to join in at the 2002 NBA Draft. James also was a wide receiver in high school. He could've been in the NFL, but he was not.
